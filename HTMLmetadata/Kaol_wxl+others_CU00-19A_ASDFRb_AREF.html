SAMPLE_DESCRIPTION:

This sample contains well-crystallized kaolinite that when mixed with trace amounts of smectite and illite spectrally mimics poorly crystalline kaolinite. The sample was collected from an altered Tertiary tuff involved in a hydrothermal event about 7.6 Ma.

END_SAMPLE_DESCRIPTION.

XRD_ANALYSIS:

Clay separate contains wxl kaolinite, smectite (trace), and illite (trace).

END_XRD_ANALYSIS.

SPECTROSCOPIC_DISCUSSION:

The spectrum has absorptions characteristic of poorly crystalline kaolinite. XRD shows only the presence of wxl kaolinite, smectite, and illite. Apparently these components have a combined spectral signature that mimics pxl kaolinite.

See: Swayze, G.A., 1997, The hydrothermal and structural history of the Cuprite Mining District, southwestern Nevada: an integrated geological and geophysical approach: unpublished Ph.D. Dissertation, University of Colorado at Boulder, 399p.

END_SPECTROSCOPIC_DISCUSSION.
