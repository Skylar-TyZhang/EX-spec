Plot Location, Dimensions, and Vegetation Sampling
 The coordinates of the southeast corner of the 2x2 m plot are 29.244555 N, 91.106463 W (in decimal degrees, datum is NAD83). Within the plot, vegetation species were identified and the percent cover of each species was visually estimated.

Spectral Measurement and Processing Methods
 This spectrum is the average of 21 spectral measurements recorded throughout the plot. These spectra were collected using an ASD Field Spectrometer 3 (FS3) in reflectance mode, relative to a Spectralon reference panel. While walking around the edges of the plot, the contact probe attachement (an artificial light source with a restricted, 1 cm diameter, field of view) was used to measure the reflectance spectra of bundles of leaves and stems. Plant stems and leaves in the top 30 cm of the canopy were bunched together. The contact probe was pressed against the optically thick bundle and a spectrum was recorded at two positions along the length of each bundle. The instrument was programmed to record 6 second averages of reflectance (thus, each recorded spectrum was an average of 60 individual measurements). The average spectrum was converted to absolute reflectance by multiplying by the reflectance spectrum of the Spectralon reference panel. The first 50 channels(0.35 - 0.40 microns) and the last 30 channels (2.47 - 2.50 microns) in the spectral measurement show relatively high levels of noise.
